Mental Health & Lifestyle Data Analysis (Excel Dashboard Project)
Today I worked on a personal data analytics project where I explored how lifestyle habits relate to mental health outcomes using Excel for data cleaning, transformation, and visualization.
The goal of this project was to transform a raw dataset into an interactive dashboard that highlights lifestyle patterns affecting mental well-being.

**Project Overview**
Using a dataset of 3,000 participants from multiple countries, I analyzed the relationship between key lifestyle factors such as sleep, work hours, screen time, exercise levels, diet type, and social interaction with mental health conditions.
The entire workflow was completed in Microsoft Excel, including:
• Data cleaning and formatting
• Creating calculated fields
• Building Pivot Tables
• Designing interactive visualizations
• Creating KPI summary metrics
• Adding slicers for dynamic filtering

** Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**
From the dataset analysis, the following lifestyle averages were observed:
• Average Sleep Duration: 6 hours per day
• Average Weekly Work Hours: 39 hours
• Average Daily Screen Time: 5 hours
• Average Social Interaction Score: 5
• Total Participants Analyzed: 3,000 individuals
These metrics provide a high-level snapshot of the population's lifestyle habits.

📈 Key Insights from the Dashboard

 1. Exercise Patterns by Gender
The dashboard reveals that exercise participation varies across genders, with female participants showing the highest exercise count, followed by non-binary participants and male participants.
This could suggest differences in lifestyle priorities or wellness behaviors amongst different groups.

2️. Happiness Levels by Gender
When comparing happiness scores across gender groups:
• Female participants recorded the highest overall happiness score
• Non-binary participants followed
• Male participants had the lowest cumulative happiness score
This insight suggests that gender-based lifestyle factors may influence overall well-being.

3️. Mental Health Conditions Distribution
The dataset highlights several reported mental health conditions including:
• Anxiety
• Depression
• Bipolar Disorder
• PTSD
• No reported condition
Among these, PTSD and anxiety appear frequently, indicating the importance of understanding the environmental and lifestyle triggers behind them.

4️. Stress Levels by Gender
Stress levels also differed between groups, with female participants showing the highest stress count, followed by non-binary and male participants.
This may reflect differences in work-life balance, social pressures, or lifestyle habits.

5️. Exercise and Stress Relationship
The visualization comparing exercise level and stress level suggests that individuals who maintain moderate to high exercise levels tend to experience lower stress levels, supporting existing research that physical activity contributes to improved mental health.

6️. Diet Patterns Across Participants
Different diet types were analyzed, including:
• Balanced diet
• Junk food diet
• Keto diet
• Vegan diet
• Vegetarian diet
Balanced diets appear to be the most common among participants, while more restrictive diets were less frequent.

7️. Age Group Mental Health Distribution
Mental health conditions were also examined across age groups. The dashboard shows varying proportions of mental health conditions across different ages, highlighting how mental health challenges can affect individuals at different life stages.

Here are 3 powerful insights I came up with:
 Insight 1: Sleep and Lifestyle Balance
The dataset shows an average sleep duration of 6 hours, which is slightly below the recommended 7–9 hours.
Combined with:
39 average weekly work hours
5 hours of daily screen time
This may indicate that modern digital lifestyles and work commitments could be reducing sleep quality, which is a known factor affecting mental health.

 Insight 2: Exercise and Stress Relationship
The dashboard suggests a relationship between exercise levels and stress levels.
Participants with moderate to high exercise levels appear to experience lower stress, supporting research that regular physical activity contributes positively to mental well-being.
This highlights how lifestyle choices can play a role in managing stress and improving mental health outcomes.

 Insight 3: Gender Differences in Mental Health Patterns
The analysis shows differences across gender groups in:
Exercise participation
Stress levels
Happiness scores
Female participants recorded the highest stress counts but also higher happiness scores, which may suggest that social interaction or lifestyle behaviors influence emotional well-being differently across groups.
This demonstrates why segmenting data by demographic factors is important in behavioral and health analysis.
